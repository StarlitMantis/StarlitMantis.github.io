Entering Piecewise Functions for the TI-84 Plus

Home

Piecewise function used in the example^^^

a. Click ‘y=’ and then enter your first piece of the piecewise function in parentheses.

b. Input a division sign and then an opening parenthesis. Entering the interval is a little tricky. If we are going to enter a single argument like x>3, then we can do so with no extra hassle (x>3), but if we would like to enter something with multiple arguments, say -12<x<1 we would have to enter it as: (x>-12 and x<1) The ‘and’ is very important and is found by clicking ‘test’ (under ‘math’) and then using the arrow keys to go to the right to “logic”. You can find symbols like < and = by clicking ‘test’ (under ‘math’)

c. We can now repeat steps a and b to enter the rest of our piecewise function.

d. Click ‘graph’ and see your beautiful piecewise function!

FAQ:

Why doesn’t my graph show the whole piecewise function? You may need to mess with the zoom a little bit to get the whole graph to fit. Try clicking ‘zoom’ and then scrolling to “ZoomFit”. This will work for the graph most of the time. Sometimes, especially when one axis is much larger than the other, the ZoomFit option will not work so you may need to adjust your window size by hand. Click ‘window’ and then you can change your Xmin and Xmax, and your Ymin and Ymax. To figure out what values to enter, consider using the table function we learned about in class to find possible maximums and minimums.

Why did my calculator throw a SYNTAX error?

This can be caused by a number of reasons like putting a bad operator but more often than not it is caused by people entering subtraction sign instead of a (-). There is a difference, the (-) is for negative numbers and is right below the number 3 key. The subtraction sign is for subtraction, but if used as a negative sign, it will throw an error.
