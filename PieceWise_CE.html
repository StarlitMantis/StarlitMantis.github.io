PieceWise Functions for the TI-84 Plus CE

(Note: this way of using piecewise functions is only supported on software versions 5.8+. If you’re calculator is not on this version you can talk to me in class about updating your calculator or use the TI-84 Plus method, found here)

Piecewise function used in the example^^^

a. Click ‘y=’ and then click the arrow key to the right to make sure you are able to input values into the function. (You can test this by entering a number and if nothing pops up you know you are in the wrong spot. Make sure to delete anything you enter before continuing on)

b Click ‘math’ and then scroll all the way to the bottom until you see “piecewise(“

c. Select the number of equations in your piecewise function by clicking the respective arrow keys, then click ok with the arrow keys. In our example, we will be using a 3 piece piecewise function.

d. Enter your first equation

e. Use the arrow keys to select the box to the right of the equation. This is where we will enter our interval. You can find symbols like < and = by clicking ‘test’ (under ‘math’).

f. Enter all your equations by repeating steps d and e. Click ‘graph and see your beautiful piecewise function!

FAQ:

Why doesn’t my graph show the whole piecewise function?

You may need to mess with the zoom a little bit to get the whole graph to fit. Try clicking ‘zoom’ and then scrolling to “ZoomFit”. This will work for the graph most of the time. Sometimes, especially when one axis is much larger than the other, the ZoomFit option will not work so you may need to adjust your window size by hand. Click ‘window’ and then you can change your Xmin and Xmax, and your Ymin and Ymax. To figure out what values to enter, consider using the table function we learned about in class to find possible maximums and minimums.

Why did my calculator throw a SYNTAX error?

This can be caused by a number of reasons like putting a bad operator but more often than not it is caused by people entering subtraction sign instead of a (-). There is a difference, the (-) is for negative numbers and is right below the number 3 key. The subtraction sign is for subtraction, but if used as a negative sign, it will throw an error.
